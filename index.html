Little Story

In the heart of Melbourne, on a bustling street lined with cafés and boutiques, there was a little sushi shop that many locals had come to love. "KAI DINING" was its name, a modest establishment owned by SOE MOE KYAW and his friends , a Burmese immigrant who had moved to Australia years ago. He had fallen in love with Melbourne's vibrant culture and diverse food scene and decided to bring a piece of his homeland to share with the city.

SOE MOE KYAW had spent his youth apprenticing under a renowned sushi master from experienced japnese sushi master. Years of dedication had taught him not only the craft but the philosophy behind sushi—simplicity, precision, and respect for ingredients. When he first opened Sushi shop in the trendy suburb of Fitzroy, Melbourne, it was just a small, quiet shop. Over time, word spread about Hiroshi’s delicate, melt-in-your-mouth sashimi, and his perfectly balanced maki rolls. His specialty was a tuna nigiri, a dish so carefully prepared that customers would often marvel at its freshness and texture.
